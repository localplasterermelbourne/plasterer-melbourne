FAQ

Depending on the type of job, different materials and tools may be used for plastering. Plasterers use a combination of sand and cement to create a strong base with gypsum and other minerals added for a smoother finish. They also use trowels, hawk boards, and wet stone masonry to apply plaster to walls and ceilings or to fix any imperfections. Plasterers also utilize various adhesives and sealants to ensure the highest quality results.

A plasterer applies coats of plaster to surfaces to prepare them for painting and decorating. This process involves using a variety of tools such as trowels, floats, and hawk boards to create an even surface. A plasterer can also be employed to texture walls or ceilings with specialty finishes, like stipple and sand swirls. Our team of experienced plasterers can help you achieve the perfect finish for your home or business property.

The time it takes to plaster a room depends on the size of the room, the number of walls to be plastered, and any other special requirements. Generally speaking, an experienced plasterer can plaster a standard-sized room in anywhere from two to five hours. However, more complex jobs will take longer. When you contact us we’ll be happy to provide you with a more accurate estimate based on your needs.

The amount of time it takes for plastering to dry depends on a few factors including the climate, humidity, and ventilation in the area. Generally, the plaster will take anywhere from two to six hours to dry completely. To ensure that your newly plastered area is ready for painting or other activities, we recommend waiting at least 24 hours before covering the surface with anything else.
